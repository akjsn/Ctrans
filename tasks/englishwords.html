Analyzing, Replacing, Inserting, and Deleting English Words

Spring 2018: Question 25 (use C language to write this program)

In this program, users first input an English sentence, with each word separated with a space. After that, 2 words P and Q are input.

This exercise requires you to build a program in C to do the following operations on the sentence:

  1. Replace any occurrence of word P in the sentence with Q, and output the modified sentence.
  2. Add word Q before any occurrence of P in the sentence, and output the modified sentence.
  3. Remove any occurrence of P in the sentence, and output the modified sentence.
  4. Analyze the sentence and find out how many times every word appears in the sentence, with the results sorted, based on frequency, from high to low.
    If more than one word has the same frequency, sort alphabetically from A-Z. Note that uppercase letters (A, B, C, ...) takes precedence over lowercase letters (a, b, c, ...).

Input instructions: Input three lines of data.

  1. The first line is the sentence.
  2. The second and third lines represent the two words P and Q.

Output instructions:

  1. For the first line, output the sentence with any occurrences of P replaced with Q.
  2. For the second line, output the sentence with Q added in front of any occurrence of P.
  3. For the third line, output the sentence with any occurrence of P omitted in the output.
  4. From the fourth line onward, output the frequency of every word in order.
    Each line shows the word, and how many times the word appears in the sentence, separated with a space.

This is a book That is a cook
