Bingo

Spring 2018: Question 14 (use Python language to write this program)

We are playing a two-player bingo. Each player has a 3-by-3 grid containg numbers N1, N2, ... , N9.

Note that if Ni < Nj when i < j and Ni > Nj when i > j, then i <= Ni, Nj <= 9, 1 <= i, and j <= 9.

Three numbers C1, C2, and C3 are then chosen. Note that if Ci <> Cj when i <> j, then i <= Ci, Cj <= 3, 1 <= i, and j <= 3.

There are three outcomes from this game: Player 1 wins, Player 2 wins, or the match ended in a draw. If the three numbers form a straight line in a player’s bingo grid horizontally, vertially, or diagonally, then the player wins.

Input instructions:

  1. For every test case, input consists of three lines.
  2. First, input two lines of 9 numbers, with each number separated by a space.
    The first line is the bingo card of Player 1, and the second line is the bingo card of Player 2.
  3. Next, input 3 numbers (also separated with a space). These numbers represent the picked numbers.
  4. The numbers for inputs in (2) and (3) must be an integer in the range of 1-9, and all numbers must be different.
  5. After every test case, input 0 to continue. To end the program, input -1.

Output instruction: Every line of output represents the outcome of every test case:

  • If Player 1 wins, output “Player1 wins”.
  • If Player 2 wins, output “Player2 wins”.
  • If both players win or no one wins, output “Draw”.

Sample Input Expected Output
1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8 9
9 8 7 6 5 4 3 2 1
1 2 3
0
1 4 2 5 6 7 3 8 9
9 8 4 7 6 3 5 2 1
2 7 9
0
9 4 2 5 6 1 3 8 7
9 8 4 3 6 7 5 2 1
9 4 8
-1 		Draw
Player1 wins
Player2 wins
