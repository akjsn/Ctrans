About

This course:
1. is designed to familiarize students with the basics of computer programming languages,
2. introduces students to the concepts and methods of computer programming,
3. puts emphasis on logical thinking, program design training, and applications of program design in information engineering,
4. trains students about the process of program design, putting emphasis on the basics of software engineering such as: data structures, program testing, and debugging, and
5. establishes a foundation on professional program design.

What To Expect

Here are some things to expect during the semester...

IDE (Integrated Development Environment)

The IDE used for this course is Code::Blocks for C, and Notepad++ with Python Anaconda installed for Python. Kuolao will use these IDEs to demonstrate code in class.

You can use any other IDE, as you see fit.

Teaching Assistant Sessions

Typically, new assignments come out every Wednesday and is due the following Wednesday.

Every Wednesday evening at 18:30, you can come to the teahing assistant (TA) sessions. In these sessions, Kuolao’s TAs will explain you about the week’s assignments and assist you with any doubts. It is strongly encouraged for you to take part in the TA sessions, as the TAs will help get you one step closer to solving your weekly assignments.

Flipped Classroom Sessions

Kuolao will have several flipped classroom sessions. For this, you will need to use an app called Edmodo (available in Apple App Store, Google Play, Microsoft Store, and also on the web).

In a flipped classroom session, you will first learn about the course material outside the classroom (through videos that are given by Kuolao on Edmodo), and then in the classroom, Kuolao will give you several problems, and you will be asked to solve it. You will be grouped with other classmates for the flipped classroom sessions, so that you can discuss with your teammates on how to solve the problem.

Onboard Examinations [What to expect]

During the midterm and final exam weeks, you will face Kuolao’s 3-hour onboard examinations. These exams will cover the topics you have learned over the first or second half of the semester.

You will have to use Code::Blocks to code some programs during the examination. The examination questions are typically taken from homework exercises, so it is essential to complete the homework assignments given by Kuolao.

Statistics show that many students fall out in this course because of this, so knowing how to solve the programming problems and the logic behind them are critical to surviving the onboard examinations.

If you have any questions...

You can also ask your classmates for advice on how to build the program (but don’t ask them to make the program for you!). Feel free to contact Kuolao or any of his TAs for help. They will be ready to help you whenever you need them.
